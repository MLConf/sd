Conference Program

8:30am Registration and Coffee & Tea!
Here’s a section in the program that isn’t a talk. Notice that the title is styled differently than the ones for talks.
9:00am Interesting Title of a Talk
Dr. Christopher Bishop (University of California, Berkeley)
In this talk, I will discuss the recent advances in machine learning, with a focus on deep learning. I will also discuss some of the challenges that remain in the field.
9:25am Interesting Title of a Talk
Dr. Yoshua Bengio (University of Montreal)
In this talk, I will discuss the potential impact of machine learning on society. I will argue that machine learning has the potential to solve some of the world's most pressing problems, such as climate change and poverty. However, I will also discuss the potential risks of machine learning, such as the possibility of job displacement and the misuse of machine learning for malicious purposes.
9:50am Interesting Title of a Talk
Dr. Fei-Fei Li (Stanford University)
In this talk, I will discuss the future of machine learning. I will argue that machine learning is still in its early stages of development, and that there is still much potential for innovation in the field. I will also discuss the challenges that need to be overcome in order to realize the full potential of machine learning.
